Most "full-stack" really means *some* of the stack. For the products I build, it
means all of it: the brand, the frontend, the backend, the AI integration, and
the deployment — one person, every layer.

## Why solo

Working alone removes the handoffs. There's no spec lost in translation between
design and engineering, because they're the same conversation. A decision about
the model prompt, the database schema, and the button copy can all happen in the
same hour.

## How it ships

1. **Discovery & strategy** — understand the problem and what "shipped" means.
2. **Design** — brand, UI, and flows, built together.
3. **Build & ship** — frontend, backend, AI, deployment.
4. **Iterate** — measure against real usage and keep shipping.

That loop produced Blog Assist, HireUltra, Transpent, and Mimries — four very
different products, same builder.

> The goal was never to do everything. It was to remove everything between an
> idea and a thing real people can use.
